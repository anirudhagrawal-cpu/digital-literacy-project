# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is one of the most common types of cybercrime in India, especially among students and people who frequently use digital payment applications. In this type of fraud, criminals try to steal money by tricking users into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or by sending fake payment links.

A common method used by fraudsters is sending a fake message or phone call pretending to be a bank official, online seller, or customer support executive. The victim is asked to click a payment link or scan a QR code. Many people believe that scanning a QR code will help them receive money, but instead it allows the fraudster to withdraw money from the victim’s bank account. In some cases, students also receive fake internship or job offers and are asked to pay a small registration fee through UPI, which results in financial loss.

College students and first-time digital payment users are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of cyber safety practices. The consequences of such fraud include financial loss, stress, and misuse of personal information. In many cases, the victim realizes the fraud only after the money has already been transferred.

This case study shows the importance of digital awareness and the need to follow safe online payment practices at all times.
